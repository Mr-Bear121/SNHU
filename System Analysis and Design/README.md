Briefly summarize the DriverPass project. Who was the client? What type of system did they want you to design?

     The owner of DriverPass wanted a website to help train drivers to pass their driving tests. He believes that he can develop a better system to help drivers pass their exams.
     The client is the owner and he wants the system to be able to register students for a course. He wants us to design the system where a student can create an account, select a course package
     , take online exams, and get road training for instructors. The owner will have access to all accounts students and employees.

What did you do particularly well?

     I believe that I did well with the completeness of the requirements. It is important to make sure that any system you design is precise and complete as to facilitate
     communication between all parties involved.

If you could choose one part of your work on these documents to revise, what would you pick? How would you improve it?

     I would revise my non-functional requirements. Non-functional requirements are hard to pick since they are not obviouse. However, given time to explore them,
     You can find a plethura to include.

How did you interpret the user’s needs and implement them into your system design? Why is it so important to consider the user’s needs when designing?

     I made sure that I first understood what was being asked of me. Once I understood I listed them in simple terms and then broke them down into actions that need to be taken
     from a technology perspective. It is important to consider the user`s needs since they are your audience. If you do not take in the user`s needs then you will create a terrible product.

How do you approach designing software? What techniques or strategies would you use in the future to analyze and design a system?

     I would employ the stratagy of diagramming out who will actually using this application. If you know who is going to use this application, Then you can
     tailor the needs and experience to their expectations and create the best product for them. For example, IT requires different tools than a Customer.
